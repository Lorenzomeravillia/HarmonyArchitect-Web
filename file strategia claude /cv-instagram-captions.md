## POST 4 — Accompagna Video 4 "Follow the Voice"

Chords aren't blocks. They're voices moving together.

In a ii-V-I, the third of the ii chord resolves down to become the seventh of the V... which resolves down to become the third of the I.

That's not three separate events — it's one voice telling a story across three chords.

ClearVoicing lets you solo that voice and hear the story. Then you put all voices back together, and suddenly you hear harmony in 3D.

This is voice leading. This is how arrangers and composers actually think.

## POST 6 — Standalone "The Science"

Why does ClearVoicing work?

It's called timbral stream segregation.

When all notes share the same timbre (piano), your auditory cortex groups them as one stream — one blob.

When each note has a different timbre (bass, trumpet, horn, flute), your brain creates separate perceptual streams. You hear four voices, not one.

It's the same reason you can follow the cello in an orchestra even when 80 instruments play at once. Your brain is already wired for this.

ClearVoicing just applies this principle to ear training.

## POST 7 — Standalone "Color Coding"

Blue = Root.
Green = Third.
Amber = Fifth.
Orange = Seventh.
Pink = Extensions.

Every note on the ClearVoicing staff is color-coded by harmonic function. Not by pitch — by role.

So when you see a green dot, you know it's the third — before you even hear it. Your eyes and ears work together.

This isn't decoration. It's cognitive scaffolding. Two channels of information reinforce each other.

See the chord. Hear the chord. Understand the chord.
